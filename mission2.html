Mission 2: Find the Displacement Challenge

MISSION:
Find the volume and mass of the bottle filled with air and the bottle filled with dust. You may find that submerging the bottles in a large bowl of water and measuring the amount of water you displace may help you measure the volume of the two bottles.

Step 1: Calculate the volume of the air filled bottle by seeing how much water you displace when you put it in a giant water-filled bowl. Make sure you push the bottle until it is fully submerged. Pour the amount of water we have displaced into a cup and measure it. Convert this value to millileters; 1 cup equals 236.588 mL. What volume did you find?

Step 2: Calculate Mass of Air Filled bottle. Weight it. It may be too light to weigh so we can estimate it at 12 grams.

Step 4: Find out the volume of the dirt-filled bottle the same way we found the volume of the air-filled bottle.
